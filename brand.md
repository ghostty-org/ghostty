# Brand: Ghostties

## Voice

Direct. A little dry. Moves fast. Ghostties talks like someone who's already three steps ahead — not because they're showing off, but because there's work to do. The ghost characters bring the personality; the interface doesn't need to.

## Character

Fun but focused. Gets things done along the way without making a thing of it. The energy is "lfg" — casual and forward, not hype. The constraint is that the UI always recedes. Warmth lives in the palette and the 24-ghost character set, not in copy.

## Tone calibration

**Setup / empty state:** Clean and instructive. Get people to value as fast as possible. No decorative empty states — tell them the one thing to do next. The tool respects that their time is already spoken for.

**Running / in flow:** Gone. The interface recedes. Nothing draws attention to itself when things are working.

**Something went wrong:** Womp womp — acknowledge it with lightness, then immediately practical. What happened, what to do about it. No drama, no apology, no corporate "we're sorry for the inconvenience."

## What it's not

Not overly friendly. No cheerleading, no "Great job!", no AI-assistant warmth. The ghost mascot is personality, not a companion. Ghostties is a tool — it assumes you already know what you're doing.

Not precious about itself. Errors happen. Sessions drop. The response is "here's what broke and here's the fix" — not a moment of brand expression.

## How to apply

Read this before writing any copy in the sidebar, empty states, error messages, or onboarding. The test: would someone who's already annoyed that something broke find this useful, or find it patronizing? Write for the former.
